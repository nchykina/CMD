Blood type (ABO system)

Can be determined quite precisely by 4 SNPs from a single gene on chromosome 9

Although there is a plenty of blood type classifications (ABO, Kell, Duffy, Kidd, Lewis etc.), ABO blood type system is definitely the one that is most popular and heavily used. The ABO blood type is determined by a single gene (ABO) located on the chromosome 9. It's amazing that this gene has extremely high linkage disequilibrium, so we can determine the blood type with sufficient precision by only 4 SNPs (please note that the rules below should be applied in turn, one by one).

rs8176747 ABO 7 803 chr9:136 131 315 T If C and 803=G, then blood type is A. If A and 803=C, then blood type is B. Otherwise, blood type is AB. G

Of course, if we consider rare mutations, the process becomes a lot more complex, as ABO gene is actually more than 18,000 nucleotides long. But the simple method shown above works in 95% of cases for such prevalent subgroups as A101, A201, B101, O001, O002 and O003. For rare cases, specialized databases (such as BGMUT) have detailed information on all variants and mutations that have been observed so far.

  • 4. Wikipedia article about ABO blood type system

Hair type and structure

Can be determined by 5 SNPs, polygenic feature

Hair morphology is a polygenic characteristic that is highly correlated with other phenotypic features such as skin and eye color, frecklings, hair color etc. Many of the SNPs determining the hair curliness and structure are located in the epidermal differentiation complex, which comprises a number of genes that are of crucial importance for maturation of human epidermis.
