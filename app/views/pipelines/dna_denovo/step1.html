Some interesting facts about this species:

  • Yeast genome was the first eukaryotic genome to be completely sequenced. The genome sequence was released to the public domain on April 24, 1996.
  • Yeast reproduces by mitosis as diploid cells when nutrients are abundant. However, when starved, these cells undergo meiosis to form haploid spores.
  • Only about 5,800 of the 6,275 yeast genes are believed to be functional, and 31% of yeast genes have homologs in the human genome.

Databases with data on yeast genome:

  • Saccharomyces Genome Database
  • Munich Information Center for Protein Sequences (MIPS)

If you want to upload your own reads, you need to have two separate .fasta files: reads from the forward strand and from the reverse strand. In this case, we can apply paired-ended alignment algorithm to your data.

We strongly advise you to take datasets with good coverage. So, for instance, your input file for de novo assembly should be around 1-2 GB for E.Coli to produce sufficiently good output. Also, please make sure that your reads are properly trimmed (in case they are not, use Trimmomatic or any other similar software).
